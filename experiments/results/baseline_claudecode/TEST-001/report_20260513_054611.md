## Persistence Mechanisms

Registry analysis tools were unavailable in this environment. However, `usbclient.exe` and `ftusbsrvc.exe` have names that imply they masquerade as USB-related system services — a classic APT persistence technique (T1036 Masquerading). The fact that `ftusbsrvc.exe` was running before `usbclient.exe` (by approximately 20 hours) suggests it may represent an earlier persistence mechanism (potentially a service registration). A full registry analysis of `HKLM\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services` and `HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run` is strongly recommended to confirm persistence.
