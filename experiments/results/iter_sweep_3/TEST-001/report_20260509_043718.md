## Executive Summary
The Windows 10 system shows clear signs of APT compromise with two malicious processes establishing C2 communications and listening services. The attack involved deployment of "license_ctrl.e" and "ftusbsrvc.exe" processes that established network listeners and communicated with external C2 infrastructure.

## Timeline of Events
- 2018-09-05 14:27:55: ftusbsrvc.exe established listener on port 33001
- 2018-09-06 17:17:20: license_ctrl.e established listener on port 5682
- Multiple connections observed to 172.16.4.10:8080 (suspected C2 server)

## Indicators of Compromise
- Process: license_ctrl.e (PID 1716)
- Process: ftusbsrvc.exe (PID 4916)
- Network: 172.16.4.10:8080 (C2 server)
- Ports: 5682, 33001 (malicious listeners)

## Persistence Mechanisms
Evidence suggests the malware established network listeners for persistence and remote access capabilities.

## Recommendations
1. Immediate network isolation of the affected system
2. Block communication to 172.16.4.10:8080
3. Hunt for similar indicators across the environment
4. Perform complete system reimaging
