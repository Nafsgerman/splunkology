## Executive Summary

The system has been compromised by Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) malware with active command and control (C2) communications. Two malicious processes are running: `license_ctrl.e` (PID 1716) and `ftusbsrvc.exe` (PID 4916), both listening on unusual network ports. Multiple connection attempts to external C2 infrastructure at 172.16.4.10:8080 confirm ongoing malicious activity.

## Persistence Mechanisms

The malware has established network-based persistence through:
- Two separate malicious processes running simultaneously
- Network listeners on ports 5682 and 33001 for potential backdoor access
- Active C2 communication channel to 172.16.4.10:8080

## Recommendations

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Isolate the compromised system (172.16.5.25) from the network
2. Block C2 IP address 172.16.4.10 at network perimeter
3. Terminate processes PID 1716 and PID 4916
4. Scan for additional compromised systems communicating with 172.16.4.10

**Investigation:**
1. Analyze malware samples from processes license_ctrl.e and ftusbsrvc.exe
